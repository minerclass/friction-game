A calibrated curriculum preserves productive cognitive struggle (desirable difficulties) while dismantling exclusionary systemic barriades.

Friction is not a mechanical bug to be smoothed away by automated text engines. It is the core resistance that makes human understanding durable. By differentiating productive struggle (noetic, rhetorical, and existential work) from exclusionary structural obstacles (transcription fatigue or language barriers), educators can integrate AI as a critical partner rather than a bypass conduit.

The Pedagogical Friction Framework

Dimension Locus Preserves Threatened By (Bypass) Preservation Strategy
Noetic Friction The Head Internal cognitive labor & Piagetian schema accommodation Noetic displacement: instant output mutes the signal of "not knowing" Require handwriting/drafts before AI use; grade thinking trajectories
Rhetorical Friction The Room Dialogic struggle of defending claims to other minds Rhetorical saturation: agreeable AI conversational partners replace peers Socratic seminars, peer critiques, unpredictable human dialogue panels
Existential Friction The World Embodied accountability and personal authorial stakes Existential abstraction: claims detached from an accountable claimant Oral defenses, authorial stance requirements, real stakes in presentations
Infrastructural Friction The System Policies, grading criteria, and time structures allowing struggle Technical compliance: policies focus only on data security & detection Friction-friendly policies; grading rubrics focused on process, not just final proxy
